What were the instructor's strengths? Weaknesses?

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Really enjoyed the opportunity to learn about Kurosawa's body of work, but class discussions were hit-or-miss (sometimes understructured, would move on too fast from interesting stuff). Three hours class felt too long.
Professor Solovieva was very knowledgable and passionate about Kurosawa and film, which contributed much to the class. The weaknesses of instructor did, however, cause some issues for this student. Professor Solovieva could be very prescriptivist about what she was looking for and what she expected on assignments. Some times too much guidance was provided (avoid adjectives) but, more often, as on the midterm, expectations were unclear. The example and what we discussed of the midterm in class did not reflect the assignment we received. The example on the Yale website was purely an analysis of film techniques and stylistic elements, while the assignment included a question about the way in which the film was adapted from Lower Depths. It felt unfair to be penalized (grade-wise) and reprimanded in the next class for not knowing how to approach this assignment type we had never scene before and was not explained to us. Because we only received this prompt a week in advance of the due date, there was little time to seek advice.
Olga was helpful in pushing students to extract information from the films/texts that may have otherwise been overlooked. She could sometimes be a bit unreasonable in her expectations for papers, and feedback/grading sometimes felt arbitrary. Additionally, it sometimes felt as if she were a little rigid in her interpretation of the texts, and didn't encourage or entertain unique interpretations.

What, if anything, what would you change about this course and why?

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Given the emphasis on formal film analysis (something I don't/didn't have a background in), this course should be listed as cinema / media studies, not comparative literature.
For the midterm, a clearer explanation and maybe even an example of a successful paper (in advance of the due date). The readings were often difficult to access (only available on physical reserve in the library) or not available in the Co-op for purchase (the books seemed to have been ordered late). Easier access to the course material would have allowed me to have more time to spend deeply engaging with the readings, rather than in searching for them.
Replace some of the source texts with mere sections from said texts, in order to lighten the load of weekly reading, which sometimes seemed excessive. I would also add a little more structured lecture to the beginning of class, to facilitate discussion and provide a trajectory for the class. This was a 3 hour class, and sometimes discussion would peter out 90 minutes in, without a clear lesson plan to fall back on.
