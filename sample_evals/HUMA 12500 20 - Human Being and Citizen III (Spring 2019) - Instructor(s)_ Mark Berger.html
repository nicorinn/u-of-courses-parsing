Prof. Berger does a great job facilitating discussion as his questions about the reading assignment are pushing students to clearly see the bigger emphasis on the writer's theme. He is also very approachable to talk to in lecture, helps you want to improve your answer, not make you feel dumb.
Asks very challenging questions and pushes you to analyze the texts more closely. Very knowledgeable about each of the authors, and explains philosophical concepts in an understandable and engaging way. Also unifies the course themes in a way that makes it clear why we're reading each of the books and what perspectives they can offer.
He would ask interesting questions about the texts that would spark actual discussion over the meaning of them, not just checking questions to see if we read. He was also really efficient when it came to the denser philosophical texts, at explaining them but also challenging us to think deeper on the meaning of the text. I think it was a nice balance of discussion and guidance of the discussion.
