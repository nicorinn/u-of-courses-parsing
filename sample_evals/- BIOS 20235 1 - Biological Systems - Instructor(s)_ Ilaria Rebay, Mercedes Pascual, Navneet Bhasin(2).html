Please share any advice you have for students who are considering taking the course.

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Do not be scared by the first weeks. As long as you are making an effort to learn, it gets better. Get a study group and start the assignments early.
Rebay psets are long and you need to start them right away. Do not take this class if you cannot commit to the timing or if you are not interested in research (if you are interested in research this is a great class)
The systems biology portion of the course will be easy –– do not mistake that for easy exams. Make sure to know case studies well.

The second portion of the course may be the most challenging course I have taken, ever. I took AP Bio during a pandemic year and the course got cut short, which may have hampered my prior knowledge of genetics. If you've had no exposure, it would be a good idea to review recombination mapping and simple crosses, as well as any other basic genetic material you can get your hands on (what a balanced stock is, etc...) otherwise you will be playing what feels like catch–up during an extremely faced paced course; however, by the end, you'll definitely be better off for making it through and succeeding.
take this course only if you like biology and genetics, because it's a lot of work
This class is so hard. Never in my life have I worked so hard and learned so much in such a short span of time. Also pay attention to the little things Rebay mentions during lectures—she will ask about them on the psets, and if you remember them, they're free points.
Be prepared. Workload is crazy.
If you liked 20234, you'll probably like this even more.
Work in advance and be proactive
As someone who struggled a bit to consume the material as quickly as some other students in the course, it is incredibly helpful to write out your crosses and your reasoning for why you have arrived at your conclusions. Being able to discuss your thoughts and reasoning with other students as well is extremely helpful.
