Difficult class. Be prepared for the finals.
The labs were a bit too debug heavy imo. A lot of it wasn't really testing whether you understood the concepts and more about if you could just match the behaviour of the reference simulator.
Lectures and graded work were very, very disconnected.
The lab writeup, in general, is too short. I need to ask the TA a lot of questions until I figure out what the simulator is behaving.
Tests were difficult. Be prepared for that.
Labs (the only assignments) had little to do with the actual lectures.
The final had nothing to do with the assignments. We had no review sheet. We were tested on mostly 30 minutes from one lecture. And, the final was a horrible exam anyway (confusing and specific).
It was hard to feel excited for this course, as someone who didn't specifically have much interest in computer architecture.
The labs for this course are generally partners, so make sure to choose a partner at your skill level. My partner was a much better programmer than me, and we unfortunately gravitated to him doing the majority of the work, which was detrimental to later labs where my grasp on what I was doing was somewhat shaky. Also, I think the CS department as a whole needs to get better at giving partial credit/curving (for instance, the math department knows how to tread the fine line between the CS department and making all of its classes a joke), but that may be just me. That is, the CS department could learn a thing or two (really, a lot of things) from the math department with respect to undergraduates.
Chong's lectures were a bit disorganized and there were complaints that the final exam was a bit misaligned with lectures, since half the final was graph theory (network topology stuff) and no virtual memory/out of order execution/parallel computing was on it.

I also think that there should be a project 5 on either parallel programming or an introductory network project, since the last two weeks of lecture covered these topics.
