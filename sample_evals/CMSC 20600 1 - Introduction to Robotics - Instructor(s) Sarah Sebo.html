Lectures often introduced a new robotics problem, a new algorithm to deal with that problem, and some time to try out said algorithm ourselves. So the content felt motivated and useful.

This was probably the most well organized class I have taken in college. The entire class was in one place, the assignments were all incredibly clear with easy to follow instructions, and the TAs and prof were always readily available for office hours.
I disliked the breakout room in class exercises because they’re difficult to do over Zoom.

To be honest, I found little in this constitutive to my overall CS education at this school. There was very little content in the lectures themselves, which I had hoped to see more of. This is a purely project–based course but the projects didn't really enable me to learn how programming with robots works in the real world. I would only recommend this class to students incredibly interested in robotics, otherwise I would not recommend. Personally it deterred me from any future robot work.
